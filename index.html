LDMReg: A Hierarchical Feature-guided Latent Diffusion Model for Multimodal Image Registration

Bo Yang, Zhaohui Jiang*, Dong Pan, Haoyang Yu, Weihua Gui
School of Automation, Central South University
*Corresponding author
Code

Abstract

Due to modality differences and inherent ambiguities (e.g., large deformations and image degradation, etc.), finding accurate correspondences is highly challenging in multimodal image registration (MIR) tasks. Limited by the encoder-decoder architecture, most existing methods primarily focus on directly learning correspondences without explicitly modeling feature priors, leading to poor performance in handling misalignments caused by modality differences and ambiguities. To address these issues, we propose LDMReg, a diffusion model for MIR tasks. Unlike traditional methods, LDMReg treats MIR as a conditional generation task, aligning and encoding multimodal features layer by layer, and using them as prior conditions to guide the diffusion process. LDMReg consists of two main modules: a denoising network and a feature guidance module. The former enhances denoising performance by replacing the UNet block with a nonlinear activation-free block. The latter encodes features and facilitates cross-domain interactions, guiding the denoising network during reverse. Additionally, we apply the diffusion model in the latent space to reduce computational cost and adopt training strategies such as using multi-resolution noise instead of traditional noise. Experimental results on four MIR tasks demonstrate that LDMReg outperforms existing methods in both quantitative and qualitative comparisons, and video application verifies its effectiveness.
