LDMReg: A Hierarchical Feature-guided Latent Diffusion Model for Multimodal Image Registration

Anonymous
Code(Coming soon)

Abstract

Due to modality differences and inherent ambiguities (e.g., geometric deformations and image degradation), multimodal image registration (MIR) tasks face significant challenges in estimating accurate flow fields. Limited by the encoder-decoder architecture, most existing methods primarily focus on directly learning the flow field (data term) without explicitly modeling feature priors, leading to poor performance in handling misalignments caused by modality differences and ambiguities. To address this, this work proposes LDMReg, a novel conditional diffusion-based framework for MIR tasks that explicitly models both the data term and feature priors. Unlike traditional methods, LDMReg treats MIR as a conditional generation task, aligning and encoding multimodal features layer by layer, and using them as prior conditions to guide the diffusion process. LDMReg consists of two main modules: a denoising network and a feature guidance module. The former enhances denoising performance by replacing the UNet block with a nonlinear activation-free block. The latter encodes features and facilitates cross-domain interactions, guiding the denoising network during reverse. Additionally, this work adopts the diffusion model in the latent space to reduce computational cost and designs training strategies such as using multi-resolution noise instead of traditional noise. Experimental results on four MIR tasks demonstrate that LDMReg outperforms existing methods in both quantitative and qualitative comparisons, and some video applications verify its effectiveness.
